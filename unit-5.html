Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students should be able to:

  • Describe traffic situations using bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, course, and speed.
  • Use future forms and conditionals to state intentions and expected passing arrangements.
  • Ask another vessel’s intentions using standard SMCP phrases.
  • State avoiding action without replacing COLREG responsibilities.
  • Transform radar information into concise spoken traffic reports.
Why this matters on board: Collision avoidance communication supports decision-making but does not replace COLREGs. Students must learn to speak precisely while still understanding their legal navigational responsibilities.

1. Unit Title and Focus

This unit develops students’ ability to describe traffic situations, translate radar information into spoken reports, clarify intentions, and communicate without replacing COLREG responsibilities.

Expanded Main Discussion: communication supports COLREGs, not replaces them

This unit must be taught with a strong safety principle: VHF communication can clarify intentions, but it cannot cancel the responsibilities required by the COLREGs. Students should use communication to share observed data, ask intentions, warn about risk, and state planned action. They must avoid making vague verbal agreements that create false security.

Students should develop the habit of translating radar and visual information into exact speech: bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, relative position, and action. Without this data, “You are close” is too weak to support safe decision-making.
