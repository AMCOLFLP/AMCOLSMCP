Exercise 5A: Collision Avoidance Fill-in-the-Blanks

Type the best answer in each blank, then press Check Answers.

1. ________. I will alter course to starboard. Hint: Marker for planned action.
2. Warning. You are running into ________. Hint: Standard warning phrase.
3. Question. What are your ________? Hint: Asking another vessel's planned action.
4. CPA means Closest Point of ________. Hint: Radar term.
5. I will pass ________ of you to maintain a safe CPA. Hint: Behind the other vessel.

Expanded Activities, Quizzes, and Knowledge Checks

How to use the interactive activities

Complete the exercises in sequence: vocabulary recognition first, grammar control second, marker decision third, and role-play production last. For Unit 5, students should repeat the role-play aloud after typing it, because SMCP is a speaking skill as well as a writing skill.

Teacher Note: These added activities provide extra assessment after the lesson. Students should complete the quiz first, then the decision check, and finally the scenario writing challenge.

Exercise 4D: Knowledge Check Quiz

2. TCPA means...
3. The give-way vessel is normally required to...
4. A stand-on vessel initially should normally...
5. VHF communication in collision avoidance...

Exercise 4E: Marker and Procedure Decision Check

Select the most accurate SMCP marker, priority signal, or operational decision for each situation.

1. You state: “Risk of collision exists.”
2. You ask: “What are your intentions?”
3. You state: “I will alter course to starboard and pass astern of you.”
4. You report: “Bearing is steady and range is decreasing.”
5. You recommend: “You should reduce speed.”

Student Self-Assessment Checklist

Using this checklist honestly

Students should tick an item only when they can perform it without looking at the model answer. If a student cannot explain why a marker is correct, the item should remain unticked and the student should review the grammar and sample conversation again.

Before moving to the next unit, tick the skills you can perform confidently.
