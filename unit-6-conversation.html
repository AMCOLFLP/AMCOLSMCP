6. Sample Conversation

Practise this conversation aloud. Pay attention to message markers, call signs, numbers, read-backs, and the words “Over” and “Out.”

Audio Practice: Unit 6 Sample Conversation

Listen to the complete onboard exchange. Practise the order–read-back–status pattern used in closed-loop communication.

Bridge: Fore station, Bridge. Question. Are you ready to heave in the head line? Over.

Fore Station: Bridge, Fore station. Answer. Ready to heave in the head line. Over.

Bridge: Instruction. Heave in the head line. Slack away the forward spring. Over.

Fore Station: Answer. Heaving in the head line. Slacking away the forward spring. Over.

Fore Station: Warning. Crew are clear of snap-back zone. Information. Head line is tight. Over.

Bridge: Instruction. Hold on head line. Over.

Fore Station: Answer. Holding on head line. Information. Forward spring is slack. Out.

Cargo Control: Deck, Cargo Control. Question. Are hatch covers secured? Over.

Deck: Answer. Hatch covers are secured and checked. Out.

Conversation analysis

The conversation demonstrates order → read-back → status report. Students should circle the action verbs and underline the objects. This helps them see whether the receiver repeated the safety-critical part of the instruction.

Listening and Pronunciation Drill: Closed-Loop Mooring Communication Drill

Student task: Listen as your teacher or partner reads the script twice. On the first listening, identify the main situation. On the second listening, write the key information: station name, vessel name, marker used, position/course/time, and action required.

Bridge: Instruction. Heave in the head line and slack away the forward spring. Over.

Fore station: Answer. Heaving in the head line and slacking away the forward spring. Over.

Fore station: Warning. Crew are clear of snap-back zone. Information. Head line is tight. Over.

Bridge: Instruction. Hold on head line. Over.

Pronunciation Focus

  • Heave in, not pull.
  • Slack away, not loosen a little.
  • All lines are made fast.
Pair Practice: Student A reads the transmission. Student B writes down the key operational details, then reads them back using SMCP style.
