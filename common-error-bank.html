Common Error Bank

This section helps students replace informal, risky, or unclear English with safer SMCP-style communication.

Risky / Informal EnglishStandard SMCP-Style CorrectionWhy It Is Better
Can you repeat that?Say again.Use the standard repetition phrase.
Go to twelve.Instruction. Change to channel one-two.Read channels as separate digits and use Instruction.
Turn right.Alter course to starboard.Use maritime direction terms.
We need help fast.Request. I require immediate assistance.Use Request and formal assistance phrase.
There is something floating there.Warning. Drifting hazard reported in position...State the hazard and position clearly.
The ropes are okay.Information. All lines are made fast.Report operational status precisely.
What are you doing?Question. What are your intentions?Use standard collision-avoidance question.
I am going behind you.Intention. I will pass astern of you.Use Intention and correct sea term.
The buoy lamp is broken.Information. Buoy light is extinguished.Use standard navigational warning wording.
OK.Answer. [Repeat the critical order].Read back safety-critical instructions.
Move forward slowly.Instruction. Dead slow ahead.Use standard engine order.
Put the ladder on the right side.Instruction. Rig pilot ladder on starboard side.Use starboard/port and operational verb “rig.”
Water is coming inside.Information. Flooding in forward store.State location and nature of problem.
I cannot see well.Warning. Visibility restricted by fog/rain.Report the cause of restricted visibility.
Please wait.Stand by.Use short standard VHF phrase.
