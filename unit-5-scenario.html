7. Core Feature: Radar-to-Speech Translation and COLREG Awareness

Radar information must be converted into precise spoken messages. A good traffic report includes target identity if known, bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, movement, and intended action. For example: “Information. Target on starboard bow, bearing zero-four-five degrees, range four decimal zero miles, CPA zero decimal three miles in twelve minutes.” This is clearer than “that ship is close.” Students must remember that VHF communication supports, but does not replace, COLREG duties. Action should be early, substantial, and clear.

Interactive Exercises

Radar-to-speech checklist

  1. Identify target vessel if known.
  2. State bearing and range.
  3. State CPA and TCPA if available.
  4. State risk using Warning if needed.
  5. Ask intentions or state your own action using Intention.
  6. Make early, substantial, and clear action according to COLREGs.

Sample Scenario Section: Sample Scenario: Crossing Situation, Radar Report, and COLREG-Aware VHF Communication

Student Task: Read the operational scenario carefully. Then answer the comprehensive questions. Your answers should connect vocabulary, grammar, SMCP markers, operational procedure, and correct maritime phraseology from this unit.

At 2215 UTC, MV Northern Cape is proceeding on course 142 degrees at 13 knots in moderate traffic. The Officer on Watch observes MV Silver Ray on the starboard bow by radar at bearing zero-four-eight degrees, range 5.2 nautical miles. The target's bearing remains nearly steady while the range decreases. ARPA indicates CPA 0.4 nautical miles in 18 minutes. AIS shows Silver Ray proceeding on course 238 degrees at 12 knots. Visibility is 5 nautical miles with intermittent rain showers.

The OOW assesses that risk of collision may exist. MV Northern Cape appears to be the give-way vessel in a crossing situation. The master is called to the bridge. The OOW prepares a short VHF call to clarify the situation, but the master reminds the team that VHF communication must support COLREG action and must not replace early and substantial avoiding action.

MV Northern Cape intends to alter course to starboard and pass astern of Silver Ray. The message must include radar-to-speech information, a warning if necessary, a question about the other vessel's intentions, and a clear statement of own intention.

Teacher Notes / Expected Answer Points
  • Students should mention steady bearing, decreasing range, CPA, TCPA, crossing situation, give-way obligation, starboard alteration, and passing astern.
  • Accept “I will alter course to starboard and pass astern of you” as a strong intention statement.
  • Answers should reinforce that COLREGs remain the legal basis for action.

How to answer the scenario questions

For this unit scenario, students should not copy long sentences from the passage. A strong answer should identify the operational problem, choose the correct SMCP marker, use the target grammar from Unit 5, and produce a safe standard phrase. Teachers can ask students to justify why they selected each marker.
