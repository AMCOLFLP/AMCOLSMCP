Final Integrated Capstone Simulation

This capstone brings together all units. Students work in teams and perform a complete maritime communication sequence.

Scenario: MV AMCOL Navigator Approaches Port During Developing Risk

MV AMCOL Navigator is approaching Rayong Pilot Boarding Ground Alpha. Visibility is reduced by rain, traffic density is increasing, and several fishing vessels are crossing near the fairway. The vessel must report to VTS, request pilot and tug assistance, respond to a NAVTEX warning about an unlit buoy, clarify a developing collision risk with another vessel, prepare the pilot ladder, and coordinate mooring stations before arrival.

During the approach, VTS instructs the vessel to maintain listening watch on channel one-two. A target vessel appears on the starboard bow with CPA zero decimal four nautical miles and TCPA one-two minutes. The master decides to reduce speed and pass astern. Later, the pilot station instructs the ship to rig the pilot ladder on starboard side two metres above water. During berthing, the foredeck reports that the forward spring is tight and crew must keep clear of the snap-back zone.

Team Roles

  • Student 1: Officer on Watch / Vessel station
  • Student 2: VTS Officer
  • Student 3: Other Vessel
  • Student 4: Pilot Station / Pilot
  • Student 5: Foredeck or Aft Station

Required Communication Tasks

  1. Routine VHF contact with vessel identity, position, ETA, and radio procedure.
  2. SECURITE/NAVTEX warning interpretation and safe-action advice.
  3. Collision avoidance exchange using bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, and Intention.
  4. Pilot boarding exchange with ladder side and height.
  5. Closed-loop mooring exchange with orders and read-backs.
  6. Final summary report using Information and Answer.
