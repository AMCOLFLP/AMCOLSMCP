Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students should be able to:

  • Decode basic NAVTEX-style abbreviations into clear spoken English.
  • Describe weather and navigational hazards using precise adjectives and passive structures.
  • Broadcast a SECURITE safety message with position, hazard, and advice.
  • Differentiate between factual Information, danger Warning, and safe-action Advice.
  • Read positions, times, wind force, visibility, and sea-state information accurately.
Why this matters on board: NAVTEX and weather warnings are often abbreviated. Students must learn how to convert short written warnings into clear spoken communication without changing the operational meaning.

1. Unit Title and Focus

This unit teaches students how to understand weather and navigational warnings, decode NAVTEX-style information, and convert abbreviated warnings into clear spoken SMCP messages.

Expanded Main Discussion: decoding information without changing meaning

NAVTEX and maritime safety information are often short, abbreviated, and data-heavy. Students must learn to expand them into spoken English while preserving the original meaning. They should not guess missing information or exaggerate the warning.

The main skill is conversion: abbreviation → hazard type → position/time → movement or weather details → advice. A correct spoken warning gives navigators enough information to assess risk and take safe action.
