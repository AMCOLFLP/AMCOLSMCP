Combined Assessments: Diagnostic Pre-Test and Comprehensive Post-Test

This page combines the starting diagnostic assessment and the final assessment in one location. Students should complete the pre-test before Unit 1 and the post-test after Unit 7 and the capstone simulation.

Start Pre-TestStart Post-Test
Teacher note: Use the pre-test as a baseline and the post-test as evidence of improvement. Students should compare their results to identify growth in vocabulary, grammar, SMCP marker selection, and operational communication.

Part 1: Diagnostic Pre-Test

This pre-test helps the teacher understand students’ starting level before they begin the SMCP course. It covers message markers, routine VHF, emergency priorities, pilotage, collision avoidance, onboard operations, and warnings.

Instruction: Complete the quiz individually. Do not worry if you do not know all answers yet. This is a diagnostic activity, not a final grade.

Part A: SMCP Diagnostic Multiple Choice

Select the best answer, then check your score.

1. Which marker should introduce an operational order from VTS?
2. Which phrase is standard for asking someone to repeat a message?
3. Which alert is used for grave and imminent danger?
4. In SMCP, “I will alter course to starboard” is best introduced by:
5. Which wording is best for radio readability?
6. What does CPA mean?
7. Which phrase reports completed mooring status?
8. Which marker is most suitable for “Visibility restricted by fog”?
9. Which word should be used instead of “right side” in formal navigation?
10. Which message priority is used for navigational warnings to all stations?

Part B: Short Diagnostic Writing

Write a short VHF exchange between your vessel and VTS. Include vessel identity, position, ETA, and one question.

Part 2: Comprehensive Post-Test

This post-test checks whether students can apply the complete SMCP module after Units 1–7.

Suggested scoring: Part A = 30 points, Part B = 20 points, Part C = 30 points. Total = 80 points.

Part A: Comprehensive Multiple Choice

Select the best answer, then check your score.

1. A vessel requests a pilot and two tugs. Which marker is correct?
2. A station reports a drifting container in a traffic lane. Which marker is correct?
3. “I will pass astern of you” is:
4. Which phrase is the best read-back?
5. Which communication priority is correct for a medical case requiring assistance but no immediate danger to the vessel?
6. Which phrase is standard?
7. What should a complete distress message include?
8. Which phrase is best for NAVTEX conversion?
9. Which marker gives a direct response?
10. Which marker recommends a safe action but is not necessarily an order?
11. Which statement uses passive status reporting?
12. Which is a correct routine ending when no reply is expected?
13. Which term means the side sheltered from wind and sea?
14. Which term means time to closest point of approach?
15. Which marker should introduce “What are your intentions?”
