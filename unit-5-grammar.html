Sentence patterns for clear operational English. Work through this page, then use the Next Page button to continue.

3. Grammar Focus

Unit Grammar Target: future intentions with will, conditional clauses with if, radar description patterns, warnings, and exact comparative language.

Collision-avoidance communication must be precise. Students need grammar that describes observed facts, predicts risk, asks intentions, and states own action without ambiguity. A VHF exchange may support collision avoidance, but it must never replace COLREG duties or good seamanship.

3.1 Using will for clear intentions

Use will + base verb after Intention to state a planned manoeuvre. This is stronger and clearer than vague phrases such as “I am thinking to turn” or “maybe I go.”

PatternExampleOperational Purpose
Intention. I will + base verbIntention. I will alter course to starboard.Declares avoiding action.
Intention. I will pass...Intention. I will pass astern of you.Clarifies passing arrangement.
Intention. I will maintain...Intention. I will maintain course and speed.States stand-on action when appropriate.
Intention. I will reduce...Intention. I will reduce speed to eight knots.States speed action.

3.2 Conditional clauses for safe coordination

Use if + present simple, will + base verb to connect another vessel’s action with your intended action. Keep the sentence short and avoid creating informal agreements that conflict with COLREGs.

  • If you maintain course and speed, I will pass astern of you.
  • If you alter course to starboard, I will maintain my present course.
  • If risk of collision remains, I will reduce speed further.
  • If you cannot alter course, report your intentions.

Student note: Conditional statements are useful, but they must not sound like negotiation against the Rules of the Road. The safest action must still follow COLREGs.

3.3 Radar-to-speech grammar patterns

Radar information should be spoken in a fixed pattern: target position, bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, and risk. This helps the listener understand the traffic picture quickly.

Information TypeGrammar PatternExample
BearingTarget is on + relative bearingTarget is on my starboard bow.
RangeRange is + number + milesRange is three decimal five miles.
CPACPA is + distanceCPA is zero decimal three miles.
TCPATCPA is + timeTCPA is twelve minutes.
RiskBearing is steady and range is decreasingYour bearing is steady and range is decreasing.

3.4 Warning grammar

Warnings must state danger directly. Use present simple for a general danger and present continuous when the danger is developing now.

  • Warning. Risk of collision exists.
  • Warning. You are running into danger.
  • Warning. You are crossing the traffic lane.
  • Warning. Your vessel is standing into shallow water.

3.5 Avoiding ambiguous comparative words

Words such as near, far, soon, left, right, and a little are risky unless supported by exact data. Use measurable language whenever possible.

VaguePrecise
You are too close.Warning. CPA is zero decimal two miles.
Turn right a little.Instruction. Alter course to starboard ten degrees.
I will go behind.Intention. I will pass astern of you.
It will happen soon.TCPA is eight minutes.

3.6 Common grammar problems in Unit 5

ProblemWeak SentenceImproved Collision-Avoidance Sentence
Using left/rightI will turn right.Intention. I will alter course to starboard.
Unclear intentionI try to pass you.Intention. I will pass astern of you.
Missing dataYou are close to me.Information. Your bearing is steady, range two decimal eight miles.
Wrong tenseI altered course now.I am altering course now. / I will alter course now.

Additional Grammar Discussion: avoiding uncertain language

In close-quarters situations, uncertain grammar can be dangerous. Phrases such as “maybe I will turn” or “I think I go behind” are not operationally strong. Use will for clear intention and exact sea terms for the action.

Weak GrammarOperational ProblemImproved Sentence
I maybe turn right.Uncertain and uses non-maritime direction.Intention. I will alter course to starboard.
You are near me.No measurable data.Information. Your bearing is steady, range two decimal five miles.
What you do?Informal question form.Question. What are your intentions?
