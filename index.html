Welcome to the Maritime English SMCP Interactive Course

This interactive training module is designed for deck maritime students to develop proficiency in the IMO Standard Marine Communication Phrases (SMCP). The course provides comprehensive, practical instruction for clear and effective communication in real-world maritime operations, including bridge, port, and shipboard scenarios.

Each unit delivers essential vocabulary, grammatical structures, and the correct application of SMCP message markers. Through realistic conversations, interactive exercises, and role-play scenarios, students will practice and master the standardized language required for safe navigation and operational efficiency.

Training Objective: To ensure that communication is clear, concise, and unambiguous, thereby enhancing safety at sea. This module aligns with the principles of the SMCP, focusing on practical application rather than theoretical knowledge alone. Students are encouraged to practice all exercises aloud to build confidence and fluency.

Course Content Summary

Unit Focus Link
Unit 1: VHF Fundamentals and Routine Traffic This unit teaches students how to establish contact, spell vessel information with the phonetic alphabet, conduct routine VHF exchanges, and perform a radio check using clear, standardized language. Go to Unit 1
Unit 2: Distress, Urgency, and Safety Communications This unit trains students to recognize and produce priority communications during emergencies. Students learn the difference between distress, urgency, and safety messages and practise organizing critical information under pressure. Go to Unit 2
Unit 3: Pilotage and Port Arrival This unit prepares students for port approach communication, pilot boarding arrangements, helm orders, engine orders, and safe coordination between bridge, pilot station, and deck crew. Go to Unit 3
Unit 4: Vessel-to-Vessel Collision Avoidance This unit develops students’ ability to describe traffic situations, translate radar information into spoken reports, clarify intentions, and communicate without replacing COLREG responsibilities. Go to Unit 4
Unit 5: On-Board Operations - Mooring and Cargo This unit focuses on internal shipboard communication during mooring, unmooring, cargo handling, and deck coordination. Students learn to use closed-loop communication and read-backs to prevent accidents. Go to Unit 5
Unit 6: Meteorological and Navigational Warnings This unit teaches students how to understand weather and navigational warnings, decode NAVTEX-style information, and convert abbreviated warnings into clear spoken SMCP messages. Go to Unit 6
Unit 7: Mastering All 8 SMCP Message Markers This unit helps students understand, choose, and use all eight IMO SMCP message markers in real maritime communication. Go to Unit 7
