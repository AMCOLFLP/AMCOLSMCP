2. Vocabulary List

Study the terms below. Focus on meaning, pronunciation, and how each word is used during real maritime operations.

TermStudent-Friendly MeaningHow It Is Used On Board
COLREGsInternational Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea.Rules that govern vessel conduct and collision avoidance.
CPAClosest Point of Approach.Minimum predicted distance between two vessels.
TCPATime to Closest Point of Approach.Predicted time until CPA occurs.
BearingDirection of an object from the vessel.Reported in degrees or relative direction.
RangeDistance from own vessel to target.Radar target may be at range 4.2 nautical miles.
Target vesselVessel being tracked or observed.Used in radar plotting and traffic reports.
Crossing situationTwo vessels approach so that risk of collision may exist.Usually one vessel is give-way and one stand-on.
Head-on situationTwo power-driven vessels meeting on reciprocal or nearly reciprocal courses.Both should normally alter course to starboard.
OvertakingApproaching another vessel from more than 22.5 degrees abaft the beam.Overtaking vessel keeps clear.
Give-way vesselVessel required to take early and substantial action.Must avoid close-quarters situation.
Stand-on vesselVessel required to maintain course and speed initially.May act if give-way vessel does not take action.
Risk of collisionSituation where bearing remains steady and range decreases.Requires assessment and avoiding action.
Alter courseChange heading of the vessel.Must be clear and early when avoiding collision.
Reduce speedDecrease vessel speed.Used to increase CPA or allow traffic to pass.
Pass aheadCross in front of another vessel.Can be dangerous if not agreed or safe.
Pass asternPass behind another vessel.Common avoiding action for give-way vessel.
Close-quarters situationVessels are too close for comfort or safety.Requires immediate attention and action.
Restricted visibilityPoor visibility caused by fog, rain, snow, sand, or other conditions.Requires safe speed and sound signals.

Vocabulary as a radar-to-speech system

Collision-avoidance vocabulary works as a sequence: identify the target, state bearing/range, assess CPA/TCPA, describe the situation, then state action. Students should practise speaking the radar picture aloud using measurable data.
