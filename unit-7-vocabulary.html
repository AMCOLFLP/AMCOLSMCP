2. Vocabulary List

Study the terms below. Focus on meaning, pronunciation, and how each word is used during real maritime operations.

TermStudent-Friendly MeaningHow It Is Used On Board
NAVTEXSystem for broadcasting maritime safety information by text.Ships receive weather, navigational, SAR, and safety messages.
MSIMaritime Safety Information.Includes navigational warnings, meteorological warnings, and SAR information.
Navigational warningMessage about hazards to navigation.Examples: drifting container, unlit buoy, wreck, obstruction.
Meteorological warningWeather warning affecting safe navigation.Examples: gale, storm, restricted visibility.
GaleStrong wind, generally Beaufort Force 8 or 9.Reported in weather warnings.
SquallSudden strong increase in wind speed.Dangerous for small vessels and deck operations.
SwellLong waves caused by distant weather.May affect rolling and cargo securing.
Sea stateCondition of sea surface.Reported as slight, moderate, rough, very rough, etc.
VisibilityDistance that can be seen.May be restricted by fog, rain, or haze.
Restricted visibilityReduced visibility caused by weather or other conditions.Requires safe speed and careful lookout.
Drifting hazardFloating object moving with wind or current.Example: container, log, fishing gear.
Unlit buoyBuoy whose light is not working.Creates danger at night or poor visibility.
WreckSunken or damaged vessel that may be a hazard.Marked or reported by navigational warning.
ObstructionObject that blocks or endangers navigation.May be charted or uncharted.
Backing windWind direction changing counter-clockwise.Important in weather interpretation.
Veering windWind direction changing clockwise.Reported in forecasts.
Forecast areaNamed sea area used in weather forecasts.Warnings apply to specific areas.
UTCCoordinated Universal Time.Used for standard timing in maritime messages.

Vocabulary as warning categories

Vocabulary should be grouped into weather terms, navigation hazards, positions/times, and advice phrases. This helps students decode messages quickly and prepare a SECURITE broadcast.

CategoryExamplesSMCP Use
Weathergale, squall, swell, visibilityWarning. Visibility restricted by fog.

Advicekeep clear, navigate with cautionAdvice. Keep a sharp lookout.
