2. Vocabulary List

Study the terms below. Focus on meaning, pronunciation, and how each word is used during real maritime operations.

TermStudent-Friendly MeaningHow It Is Used On Board
Pilot stationDesignated location where pilot boards or disembarks.Vessels report ETA and receive boarding instructions.
Pilot ladderSpecial ladder rigged for pilot boarding.Must be rigged safely and at the instructed height above water.
Combination ladderPilot ladder combined with accommodation ladder.Used when freeboard is high.
Heaving lineLight line used to pass another line.Used during pilot boarding and mooring operations.
ManropesRopes used by the pilot for support near the ladder.Rigged when required by pilot boarding instructions.
Lee sideSide sheltered from wind and sea.A vessel may make a lee for the pilot boat.
Under-keel clearanceDistance between keel and seabed.Critical during port approach and shallow water transit.
BerthAssigned place alongside a quay or jetty.Port control gives berth number and arrival instructions.
TugPowerful vessel assisting manoeuvring.May push, pull, escort, or hold the vessel.
Mooring stationDeck area where crew handle mooring lines.Fore and aft stations report readiness to bridge.
Hard-a-starboardMaximum rudder angle to starboard.Standard helm order used for immediate manoeuvre.
MidshipsRudder to center position.Used after a turn or to stop swinging.
SteadyReduce swing and maintain heading.Used during pilotage or narrow channel transit.
Dead slow aheadVery low engine power ahead.Used during close manoeuvring.
Stop engineEngine telegraph order to stop propulsion.Used to reduce speed or prepare for astern movement.
Let go anchorRelease anchor under command.Used when anchoring or emergency stop is required.
Make fastSecure a line.Used when a mooring line is put on a bollard and secured.
Let goRelease a line or anchor.Used in mooring, unmooring, and anchoring.

Vocabulary as port-arrival instructions

Students should group vocabulary into functions: pilot boarding equipment, bridge orders, tug/mooring terms, and port approach locations. This helps them build practical messages instead of memorizing isolated words.

Vocabulary GroupExamplesMessage Frame
Pilot boardingpilot ladder, combination ladder, manropesPilot ladder is rigged on ___ side ___ metres above water.
Bridge ordersmidships, hard-a-starboard, dead slow aheadInstruction. ___.
Arrival positionpilot station, berth, fairwayProceed to ___.
