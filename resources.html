Appendices and Course Resources

Clear pronunciation prevents mistakes. Practise these forms aloud, especially when reading positions, courses, times, draughts, and radio channels.

ItemStandard ReadingPractice Example
Coursethree digitsCourse zero-nine-zero.
Channelseparate digitsChannel one-six.
Decimalsay decimalDraught seven decimal five metres.
Positiondegrees and minutesLatitude one-three degrees two-five minutes North.
Timefour digitsETA one-four-three-zero UTC.
  • Identify the station called and the station calling.
  • Use message markers when the function of the message may be unclear.
  • Use standard sea terms: port, starboard, ahead, astern, bearing, range, CPA, TCPA.
  • Use “Say again” when you need repetition.
  • Read back all safety-critical orders.
  • Avoid informal phrases such as “left,” “right,” “OK,” “go there,” and “do it now.”
  • End with “Over” when you expect a reply and “Out” when the exchange is finished.

In groups of three, perform a 5-minute scenario that combines routine VHF contact, pilotage, traffic risk, and an onboard mooring or cargo report. Each student must use at least three message markers and must read back one critical instruction. The group should include a short debrief explaining what was clear and what could be improved.
