Exercise 6A: Mooring and Cargo Fill-in-the-Blanks

Type the best answer in each blank, then press Check Answers.

1. Instruction. ________ away the stern line. Hint: Reduce tension.

3. The hatch covers are ________ before sailing. Hint: Closed and locked safely.
4. Question. Are you ready to ________ in the head line? Hint: Pull in using a winch.
5. Answer. Forward spring is ________ and clear of obstruction. Hint: Safe/fastened.

Expanded Activities, Quizzes, and Knowledge Checks

How to use the interactive activities

Complete the exercises in sequence: vocabulary recognition first, grammar control second, marker decision third, and role-play production last. For Unit 6, students should repeat the role-play aloud after typing it, because SMCP is a speaking skill as well as a writing skill.

Teacher Note: These added activities provide extra assessment after the lesson. Students should complete the quiz first, then the decision check, and finally the scenario writing challenge.

Exercise 5D: Knowledge Check Quiz

Choose the best answer for each item. These questions test understanding, not memorization only.

1. Closed-loop communication means...
2. A snap-back zone is dangerous because...
3. A head line normally leads...
4. Which sentence best reports completed cargo status?
5. “All lines are made fast” means...

Exercise 5E: Marker and Procedure Decision Check

Select the most accurate SMCP marker, priority signal, or operational decision for each situation.

1. Bridge says: “Heave in the forward spring.”
2. Fore station reports: “All lines are made fast.”
3. Bosun says: “Keep clear of snap-back zone.”
4. Cargo control asks permission to start loading.
5. Aft station repeats the order: “Slacking away the stern line.”

Student Self-Assessment Checklist

Using this checklist honestly

Students should tick an item only when they can perform it without looking at the model answer. If a student cannot explain why a marker is correct, the item should remain unticked and the student should review the grammar and sample conversation again.

Before moving to the next unit, tick the skills you can perform confidently.
