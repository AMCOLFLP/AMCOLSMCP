Exercise 2A: Routine VHF Fill-in-the-Blanks

Type the best answer in each blank, then press Check Answers.

1. Bangkok VTS, this is MV Ocean Pioneer. Radio ________. Over. Hint: A request to test radio quality.
2. ________. My ETA at the pilot station is one-six-three-zero local time. Hint: Marker used when replying.
3. ________. What is your present position? Hint: Marker used when asking.
4. Readability is ________ on the one-to-five scale. Hint: Best radio quality.
5. Do not say “over and ________” together. Hint: This means no reply is expected.

Expanded Activities, Quizzes, and Knowledge Checks

How to use the interactive activities

Complete the exercises in sequence: vocabulary recognition first, grammar control second, marker decision third, and role-play production last. For Unit 2, students should repeat the role-play aloud after typing it, because SMCP is a speaking skill as well as a writing skill.

Teacher Note: These added activities provide extra assessment after the lesson. Students should complete the quiz first, then the decision check, and finally the scenario writing challenge.

Exercise 1D: Knowledge Check Quiz

Choose the best answer for each item. These questions test understanding, not memorization only.

1. Which channel should normally be used for routine work after initial contact?
2. Which phrase is the standard way to ask another station to repeat a message?
3. What does “Over” mean at the end of a VHF transmission?
4. Which message marker is best before “What is your ETA at the pilot station?”
5. In a radio check, “Readability is five” means the signal is...

Exercise 1E: Marker and Procedure Decision Check

Select the most accurate SMCP marker, priority signal, or operational decision for each situation.

1. You report your draught and ETA to VTS.
2. VTS tells your vessel to change to channel one-two.
3. You ask port control for permission to enter the fairway.
4. You reply directly to a question about your call sign.
5. You ask another station for its present position.

Student Self-Assessment Checklist

Using this checklist honestly

Students should tick an item only when they can perform it without looking at the model answer. If a student cannot explain why a marker is correct, the item should remain unticked and the student should review the grammar and sample conversation again.

Before moving to the next unit, tick the skills you can perform confidently.
