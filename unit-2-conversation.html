6. Sample Conversation

Practise this conversation aloud. Pay attention to message markers, call signs, numbers, read-backs, and the words “Over” and “Out.”

Audio Practice: Unit 2 Sample Conversation

Listen to the complete routine VHF exchange. Repeat the call signs, numbers, message markers, and correct use of “Over” and “Out.”

Vessel: Bangkok VTS, Bangkok VTS, this is Motor Vessel Ocean Pioneer, Ocean Pioneer. Call sign Hotel-Bravo-Seven-Two. Over.

VTS: Ocean Pioneer, this is Bangkok VTS. Send your message. Over.

Vessel: Information. I am entering the fairway from the south. My position is bearing one-eight-zero degrees, distance four decimal five miles from Buoy Bravo. My draught is seven decimal eight metres. Over.

VTS: Question. What is your ETA at the pilot station? Over.

Vessel: Answer. My ETA at the pilot station is one-six-three-zero local time. Request. Radio check on this channel. Over.

VTS: Answer. Readability is five. Instruction. Maintain listening watch on channel one-two. Report at Buoy Charlie. Over.

Vessel: Answer. Maintaining listening watch on channel one-two. Will report at Buoy Charlie. Out.

Conversation analysis

Notice how the vessel gives one group of facts at a time: position, draught, ETA, then request for radio check. VTS replies with the readability rating and then gives an instruction. The vessel closes with a read-back, not just “OK.” This is the model students should imitate in their own VHF scripts.

Listening and Pronunciation Drill: Routine VHF Contact and Radio Check Drill

Student task: Listen as your teacher or partner reads the script twice. On the first listening, identify the main situation. On the second listening, write the key information: station name, vessel name, marker used, position/course/time, and action required.

Bangkok VTS, Bangkok VTS, this is Motor Vessel Ocean Pioneer. Call sign Hotel-Bravo-Seven-Two. Over.

Ocean Pioneer, this is Bangkok VTS. Send your message. Over.

Information. My position is bearing one-eight-zero degrees, distance four decimal five miles from Buoy Bravo. Request. Radio check on channel one-two. Over.

Answer. Readability is five. Instruction. Maintain listening watch on channel one-two. Report at Buoy Charlie. Over.

Pronunciation Focus

  • Channel one-two, not “twelve.”
  • Draught seven decimal eight metres.
  • Bearing one-eight-zero degrees, distance four decimal five miles.
Pair Practice: Student A reads the transmission. Student B writes down the key operational details, then reads them back using SMCP style.
