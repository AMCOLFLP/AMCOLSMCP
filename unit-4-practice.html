Exercise 4A: Pilotage and Port Arrival Fill-in-the-Blanks

Type the best answer in each blank, then press Check Answers.

1. ________ the pilot ladder on the starboard side. Hint: Prepare and set up the ladder.
2. ________. What is my pilot boarding time? Hint: Marker for asking.
3. Instruction. Steer zero-nine-zero and keep the vessel ________ in the fairway. Hint: Maintain a stable heading.
4. Make a ________ for the pilot boat on the port side. Hint: Sheltered side from wind/sea.
5. The pilot ladder must be rigged one metre ________ the water. Hint: Preposition of position.

Expanded Activities, Quizzes, and Knowledge Checks

How to use the interactive activities

Complete the exercises in sequence: vocabulary recognition first, grammar control second, marker decision third, and role-play production last. For Unit 4, students should repeat the role-play aloud after typing it, because SMCP is a speaking skill as well as a writing skill.

Teacher Note: These added activities provide extra assessment after the lesson. Students should complete the quiz first, then the decision check, and finally the scenario writing challenge.

Exercise 3D: Knowledge Check Quiz

Choose the best answer for each item. These questions test understanding, not memorization only.

1. Which phrase is standard for preparing a pilot ladder?
2. Why must helm and engine orders be read back?
3. What is the lee side?
4. Under-keel clearance is the distance between...
5. Which is a standard helm order?

Exercise 3E: Marker and Procedure Decision Check

Select the most accurate SMCP marker, priority signal, or operational decision for each situation.

1. You ask the pilot station for your boarding time.
2. The pilot orders: “Dead slow ahead.”
3. Your vessel states it will make a lee on the port side.
4. You confirm: “Pilot ladder rigged on starboard side.”
5. You report: “My present draught is eight decimal two metres.”

Student Self-Assessment Checklist

Using this checklist honestly

Students should tick an item only when they can perform it without looking at the model answer. If a student cannot explain why a marker is correct, the item should remain unticked and the student should review the grammar and sample conversation again.

Before moving to the next unit, tick the skills you can perform confidently.
