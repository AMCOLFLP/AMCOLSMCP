2. Vocabulary List

Study the terms below. Focus on meaning, pronunciation, and how each word is used during real maritime operations.

TermStudent-Friendly MeaningHow It Is Used On Board
VHF radioVery High Frequency radio used for short-range marine communication.Used to contact VTS, pilot stations, port control, and nearby ships.
Channel 16International distress, safety, and calling channel.Kept clear for distress and safety traffic; routine work should move to a working channel.
Working channelA channel used after initial contact for routine communication.Example: Change to channel zero-eight for port control instructions.
Call signUnique radio identity of a vessel.Spelled with phonetic letters when needed: Delta-Echo-Five-Niner.
MMSIMaritime Mobile Service Identity, a nine-digit number for digital calling.Used in DSC and vessel identification.
IMO numberPermanent identification number assigned to a vessel.Used in formal reports and port documentation.
VTSVessel Traffic Service that monitors and assists vessel movement.Vessels report position, course, speed, and ETA to VTS.
ETAEstimated Time of Arrival.Reported to port control or pilot station.
ETDEstimated Time of Departure.Used in berth planning and traffic management.
LOALength Overall of the vessel.Important for berth assignment and tug requirements.
Draught / DraftVertical distance from waterline to keel.Reported before entering shallow or restricted water.
Air draftHeight from waterline to highest fixed point of vessel.Used when passing under bridges or overhead cables.
FairwayNavigable channel used by vessels.VTS may instruct a ship to keep to the starboard side of the fairway.
ReadabilityHow clearly a transmission is heard.Reported using the 1-5 radio check scale.
OverEnd of transmission; reply expected.Used when the speaker expects a response.
OutEnd of communication; no reply expected.Never use “Over and out” together.
Say againStandard request to repeat a message.Used instead of casual “repeat that.”
Stand byWait and remain ready to receive further communication.Used when the station cannot answer immediately.

How to master this vocabulary

Do not memorize the words only as definitions. Practise them as report-building blocks. For example, draught, ETA, call sign, course, and speed should be combined into one clear VTS report. Students should also learn which words must be spelled and which numbers must be read digit by digit.

  • Identity frame: This is Motor Vessel ___, call sign ___.
  • Position frame: My position is bearing ___ degrees, distance ___ miles from ___.
  • Status frame: My draught is ___ metres. My ETA is ___ local time.
