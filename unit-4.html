Unit 4: Vessel-to-Vessel Collision Avoidance

1. Unit Title and Focus

This unit develops students’ ability to describe traffic situations, translate radar information into spoken reports, clarify intentions, and communicate without replacing COLREG responsibilities.

2. Vocabulary List

Study the terms below. Focus on meaning, pronunciation, and how each word is used during real maritime operations.

3. Grammar Focus

Future forms and conditional clauses are used to state intentions and expected actions: “I will alter course to starboard,” “I intend to pass astern of you,” and “If you maintain course and speed, I will pass astern.” However, VHF agreements must never replace the COLREGs. Students should state intentions clearly, avoid ambiguous words such as “left” and “right,” and use exact sea terms: port, starboard, ahead, astern, bearing, range, CPA, and TCPA.

4. SMCP Marker Application

The following message markers are especially useful in this unit:

  • Warning: used when risk of collision or danger exists.
  • Question: used to ask another vessel’s intentions.
  • Intention: used to declare own avoiding action.
  • Information: used to state observed traffic facts.
  • Advice: used when recommending a safer action.

5. Say This, Not That

Use the standard phrase in the middle column. The expressions in the last column may be understood in normal English, but they are too informal, vague, or risky for maritime operations.

SituationSay This - Standard SMCP StyleNot That - Colloquial / Risky English
Stating actionIntention. I will alter course to starboard.I will turn right.
Collision riskWarning. Risk of collision exists.We may crash.
Asking intentionsQuestion. What are your intentions?What are you doing?
Radar reportInformation. Your bearing is steady and range is decreasing.You are getting closer.
Safe passingIntention. I will pass astern of you.I will go behind you somehow.

6. Sample Conversation

Practise this conversation aloud. Pay attention to message markers, call signs, numbers, read-backs, and the words “Over” and “Out.”

Vessel A: Motor Vessel Coral Sea, this is Motor Vessel Northern Light on your port bow. Over.

Vessel B: Northern Light, this is Coral Sea. Over.

Vessel A: Information. Your bearing is steady, range three decimal two miles. Warning. Risk of collision exists. Question. What are your intentions? Over.

Vessel B: Information. I am the give-way vessel. Intention. I will alter course to starboard and pass astern of you. Over.

Vessel A: Answer. You will alter course to starboard and pass astern of me. Information. I will maintain course and speed. Over.

Vessel B: Answer. Understood. I am altering course to starboard now. Out.

7. Core Feature: Radar-to-Speech Translation and COLREG Awareness

Radar information must be converted into precise spoken messages. A good traffic report includes target identity if known, bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, movement, and intended action. For example: “Information. Target on starboard bow, bearing zero-four-five degrees, range four decimal zero miles, CPA zero decimal three miles in twelve minutes.” This is clearer than “that ship is close.” Students must remember that VHF communication supports, but does not replace, COLREG duties. Action should be early, substantial, and clear.
