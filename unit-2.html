Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students should be able to:

  • Establish and close a routine VHF exchange using correct station identification, “Over,” and “Out.”
  • Spell vessel names, call signs, and difficult words using the IMO phonetic alphabet.
  • Report position, ETA, draught, course, speed, and operational status in short SMCP-style message units.
  • Perform a professional radio check using the 1–5 readability scale.
  • Select appropriate markers for routine traffic: Information, Question, Answer, Request, and Instruction.
Why this matters on board: Routine VHF traffic is the first communication skill cadets need before they can handle pilotage, warnings, emergencies, or traffic situations. Clear routine communication reduces channel congestion and prevents wrong assumptions.

1. Unit Title and Focus

This unit teaches students how to establish contact, spell vessel information with the phonetic alphabet, conduct routine VHF exchanges, and perform a radio check using clear, standardized language. It is the foundation for safe communication with VTS, port control, pilot stations, and nearby vessels.

Expanded Main Discussion: From casual speech to operational radio language

Routine VHF traffic seems simple, but it trains the habits used in every higher-risk situation later in the course. Students should understand that a routine call is not ordinary conversation. It is a controlled exchange where every part has a purpose: identifying the station called, identifying the station calling, stating the message function, giving exact information, and closing correctly.

In real operations, weak routine communication can create unnecessary channel traffic, wrong vessel identification, missed reports, or delayed instructions from VTS. For this reason, students should practise speaking slowly, reading numbers as separate digits, spelling call signs with the phonetic alphabet, and separating one idea from the next. A good routine report should be short enough to repeat and complete enough for the listener to act on.
