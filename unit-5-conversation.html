Close-Quarters Situation Between Two Vessels

6. Sample Conversation

Practise this conversation aloud. Pay attention to message markers, call signs, numbers, read-backs, and the words “Over” and “Out.”

Audio Practice: Unit 5 Sample Conversation

Listen to the complete collision-avoidance exchange. Focus on bearing, range, CPA, TCPA, warnings, questions, and intentions.

Vessel A: Motor Vessel Coral Sea, this is Motor Vessel Northern Light on your port bow. Over.

Vessel B: Northern Light, this is Coral Sea. Over.

Vessel A: Information. Your bearing is steady, range three decimal two miles. Warning. Risk of collision exists. Question. What are your intentions? Over.

Vessel B: Information. I am the give-way vessel. Intention. I will alter course to starboard and pass astern of you. Over.

Vessel A: Answer. You will alter course to starboard and pass astern of me. Information. I will maintain course and speed. Over.

Vessel B: Answer. Understood. I am altering course to starboard now. Out.

Conversation analysis

The conversation uses a safe pattern: identify the other vessel, give radar data, state warning, ask intentions, then read back the passing arrangement. Students should practise not only speaking their own intention, but also confirming the other vessel’s intention.

Listening and Pronunciation Drill: Radar-to-Speech Collision Avoidance Drill

Student task: Listen as your teacher or partner reads the script twice. On the first listening, identify the main situation. On the second listening, write the key information: station name, vessel name, marker used, position/course/time, and action required.

Information. Target on starboard bow, bearing zero-four-five degrees, range four decimal zero miles.

Warning. Risk of collision exists. CPA zero decimal three miles in twelve minutes.

Question. What are your intentions?

Intention. I will alter course to starboard and pass astern of you.

Pronunciation Focus

  • CPA zero decimal three miles.
  • TCPA one-two minutes.
  • Bearing zero-four-five degrees.
Pair Practice: Student A reads the transmission. Student B writes down the key operational details, then reads them back using SMCP style.
